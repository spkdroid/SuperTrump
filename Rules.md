Name of the Game: Super Trump

Points and Hierarchy: Biggest to Smallest as below:

Joker – Super Trump – 0 Point but the biggest and most powerful card in the game (only one card)
Jack-3 points
9- 2 Points
A- 1 Point
10- 1 Point
King- 0 Point
Queen – 0 Point
8- 0 Point
7- 0 Point
6- 0 Point
8, 7 and 6 may be included depending on the number of players. The more number of players, the greater the chance of them being included since each player will start with six cards. 7 points per suit, 4 suites in a pack and since we will play with 2 packs, the total number of points will be 56.

Trumping rules:

You can use the trump and take a trick only if you are void in that particular suit that is in play. For example, Spades is in play and only if you don’t have Spades you can use a trump and cut it and take it.
The only exception is if you have the Super Trump, in which case, you can play it any time regardless of whether you are void or not. Also, before you use the trump, the trump should be revealed or you can ask for the trump to be revealed and then use the trump and take it. For example, Hearts is the trump and it already has been revealed and you are void in Diamonds and Diamonds is in play, then you can use any Heart and take that trick. Another example, Trump is not revealed but you think Heart is the Trump and you play it thinking that you are going to get the trick. But in this case your card will not count as a trump since the trump is not revealed yet and the next person may ask for the trump to be revealed and he can use his trump (Heart) and take that trick. Only when you are void in the suit that is in play, then you can ask for the trump to be revealed.

Bidding:

Bidding minimum is 28 and maximum is 56. Whoever has a higher bid, they get to keep the trump or can say that they want to play ‘No Trump’.  Depending on the number of people, they can ask for 1 or 2 cards. For example, I might say Joker and Jack of Spades. Whoever has those cards will become my partners but they can’t say that they are my partners. As the game unwinds, you will find out who your partners are. If you are playing with a trump, then you need to place a card in that suit face down until someone asks for the trump to be revealed. Bid of 40 or above is considered ‘Honors’ and you get to lead and you can lead with a trump. A bid of less than 40 will not give you the chance to lead and even if it is your normal turn you can’t lead with a trump until it is revealed.

What is a good hand:

If you have one or more Jacks, or have a lengthy sequence without a Jack (9, A, 10 for example). In this case you can ask for Jack of that suit to be your partner. Or having a Joker is a very good hand. If you are void in one or more suits that is also considered good since you may use a trump card and cut and take that trick when the void suit is in play.

How do you play:

Once the cards are dealt and the bid is finalized and the bidder asks for one or two cards, then everyone knows whether you are on the bidding team or the opposite team. You should play accordingly. If you are part of the bidding team, try to help your bidding partner to get the trick and get the points. Generally it is a good idea to get all the trumps out early, so if you have a Jack in another suit, it won’t get cut. If you are in the opposite team, you need to give as little points as possible if you think that the bidding team is going to get that trick. Observation, listening (what cards did the bidder ask for), memory (what high cards have already gone and what are still left), watching for what cards people play which will give a clue as to whether that person likely is part of the bidding team or not, etc are some of the key things to remember.

Points Scoring:
General rule is if you lose, you get double the negative points. Bidder gets 50% of the bidding points and partners share the remaining 50% whether win or lose.

Bid 36 and won- Bidder gets 18 and partners divide the other 18
Bid 36 and lost- Bidder gets -36 and partners divide the other -36
40 or above (Honors) get double the normal points
40 and win – Bidder gets 40 and partners divide the other 40
40 and lose- Bidder gets -80 and partners divide the other -80
56 to start with – Quadruple whatever the normal score is
56 and won- Bidder gets 112 and partners divide the other 112
56 and lost- Bidder gets -224 and partners divide the other -224
Changing your bid to 56 in the middle of the game with at least 2 cards in your hand (anyone in bidding team can do this) – Triple the normal points
Initial bid 40 and changed to 56 before the end of the game
Bidder gets 84 points and partners divide the other 84
Changed the Bid to 56 and lost
Bidder gets -168 and partners divide the other -168
